Short Bio

Xiaoyu Wang is a tenure-track Assistant Professor in School of Mathematical Sciences of University of Chinese Academy of Sciences (UCAS) since Sept 2025. Prior to this, she has been a research assistant professor and post-doctoral fellow in the Department of Mathematics of The Hong Kong University of Science and Technology, working with Prof. Zhang Tong from Sept. 2022 to June 2025. From September 2020 to August 2022, she worked with Prof. Mikael Johansson at the Division of Decision and Control Systems, Royal Institute of Technology (KTH). She earned her Ph.D. in computational mathematics, from the University of Chinese Academy of Sciences & Academy of Mathematics and System Science (AMSS), CAS, China, under the supervision of Prof. Ya-xiang Yuan, in 2020. She received a B.S. degree in mathematics, School of Mathematics and Statistics, from Wuhan University, China, in 2015.

She is particularly interested in optimization algorithms and their applications in large-scale machine learning. The topics she has been working on include:

  • Stochastic Optimization Algorithms including First-Order, Second-Order (Newton, Quasi-Newton, or Trust-Region) Methods

  • Learning Rate Policies in Stochastic Optimization

  • Applications of Optimization Methods in Machine Learning

  • Bilevel Optimization
